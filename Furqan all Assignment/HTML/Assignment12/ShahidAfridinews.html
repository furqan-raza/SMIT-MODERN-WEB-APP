Pakistani cricket ‘is in ICU,’ says former captain Shahid Afridi

Compares Pakistani cricket condition to 'a critically ill patient'


The Pakistani cricket “is in ICU,” former national team captain Shahid Afridi has said
after Green Shirt’s humiliating performances in the ICC events.


“We often discuss preparations, but when an event arrives and things go wrong, we resort
to talking about fixes. The truth is, Pakistan cricket is in critical condition due to poor decisions,” Afridi said while addressing the media in a private event.

The former all-rounder slammed the Pakistan Cricket Board, comparing the country’s cricket
condition to “a critically ill patient.”

The hosts were out of the Champions Trophy in the group stage matches after losing their two
matches to India and New Zealand. Their performance was also below par in the T20 World Cup, where they lost to the USA, leading to an early exit from the tournament.

He also questioned the return of Shadab Khan to the side for the T20I series against New Zealand.
